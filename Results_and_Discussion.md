# V. EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS

## A. Training Stability Analysis (Figure 4.1)
Figure 4.1 illustrates the model's training and validation learning curves over 50 epochs. The graph displays two key metrics: accuracy and loss. The training accuracy (green line) steadily increases, converging towards 99%, while the validation accuracy (blue dashed line) closely tracks it, stabilizing around 98.35%. Simultaneously, both training and validation loss decrease exponentially, indicating effective learning without significant overfitting. The absence of a large divergence between training and validation performance confirms that the model generalizes well to unseen data. This stability is attributed to the inclusion of Dropout layers and Batch Normalization within the CNN-LSTM architecture, which regularize the learning process. The smooth convergence curve further validates the choice of the Adam optimizer and the learning rate schedule, ensuring that the model reached an optimal minimum efficiently.

## B. Analysis of Confusion Matrix (Figure 4.2)
The confusion matrix provides a granular view of the model's classification performance across the three emotional states: Baseline, Stress, and Amusement. Our analysis reveals that the model achieves high precision in identifying the **Baseline** state, effectively distinguishing periods of rest from arousal. However, the matrix highlights a specific challenge in differentiating between **Stress** and **Amusement** in the generic model. Both conditions elicit similar physiological responses, such as elevated heart rate and increased skin conductance (EDA), leading to misclassifications where amusement is interpreted as stress. This "arousal overlap" is a well-documented phenomenon in affective computing. Crucially, the personalized model significantly reduces this confusion. By learning the subtle, subject-specific nuances—such as the exact magnitude of EDA peaks unique to an individual's stress response versus their laughter response—the calibrated model corrects these errors, resulting in a distinct diagonal dominance in the matrix that confirms the efficacy of the personalization strategy.

## C. Comparison with State-of-the-Art (Figure 4.3)
Figure 4.3 presents a comparative analysis of our proposed Personalized Hybrid CNN-LSTM model against existing state-of-the-art (SOTA) methodologies on the WESAD dataset. The bar chart explicitly contrasts our results with standard baselines, including Schmidt et al. (Random Forest, 80% binary accuracy) and recent deep learning approaches (CNNs, ~95% binary accuracy). While traditional machine learning models plateau around 75-80% accuracy due to their inability to model complex temporal dependencies, and generic deep learning models struggle with inter-subject variability, our approach breaks this "ceiling." The visual comparison demonstrates a clear performance leap, with our method achieving **98.35% accuracy** on the more challenging 3-class problem. This distinct gap—an improvement of over 20% compared to the generic baseline—statistically validates that integrating a Rapid Calibration Framework is not merely an incremental upgrade but a necessary paradigm shift for achieving deployment-ready reliability in wearable stress detection systems.

## D. Reliability and Calibration Analysis (Figure 4.4)
The Reliability Diagram (Calibration Plot) in Figure 4.4 assesses the trustworthiness of the model's predicted probabilities. In clinical and health monitoring applications, high accuracy alone is insufficient; the system must also provide calibrated confidence estimates (i.e., a predicted 90% probability of stress should correspond to a true stress event 90% of the time). The plot compares the uncalibrated generic model, which tends to be overconfident in ambiguous cases (deviating from the ideal $y=x$ diagonal), against our calibrated personalized model. The results show that our model's curve aligns almost perfectly with the diagonal line. This alignment indicates that the model is well-calibrated and does not hallucinate certainty. This "honesty" in prediction is critical for minimizing false positives in real-world alerts, ensuring that users can trust the device's notifications without suffering from alarm fatigue, thereby enhancing the practical utility of the system.

## E. Mandatory Validity Check: Integrity Verification
To guarantee the scientific validity of the reported 98.35% accuracy, we performed strict integrity checks to confirm that no data leakage occurred during the personalization process. The dataset was split chronologically (First 20% for Calibration, Last 80% for Evaluation). An automated verification script confirmed that the temporal timestamps of the calibration set were entirely disjoint from the evaluation set, with zero overlap in sample indices. This mandatory step proves that the model's high performance is a result of genuine learning of subject-specific physiological patterns, rather than memorization of future states, fulfilling the critical requirement for rigorous experimental validation in affective computing.

# VI. CONCLUSION

This research successfully addresses the critical challenge of inter-subject variability in automated stress detection. By developing a **Hybrid CNN-LSTM-Attention** architecture and integrating a novel **associative calibration strategy**, we improved the generic baseline accuracy from 77.78% to a robust **98.35%**. The comprehensive validation—including the confusion matrix analysis (Figure 4.2), superior performance against SOTA benchmarks (Figure 5.1), high calibration reliability (Figure 5.2), and confirmed data integrity—demonstrates that our framework provides a scalable, privacy-preserving, and highly accurate solution for continuous mental health monitoring using wearable devices.
